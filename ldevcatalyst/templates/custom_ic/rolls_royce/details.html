Overview of the challenge

Is it feasible to get a real time indication of heat waves and provide a warning to prevent power outages? This would be the first step to build an interpretable energy supply chain model with simulation capabilities to predict the heat wave advance with reasonable accuracy in terms of both time and geography. Using this heat wave prediction data, we can understand regions in India that are prone to heatwaves.

These can indicate the expected demand fluctuations and will help to prepare an availability of energy across regions and time, coupled with an understanding of the 'fuel availability mix' thus allowing for an optimisation framework. Fuel can include renewable resources, non-renewable resources, and emerging energy sources, like hydrogen for example, with their own efficiencies, availability, potential, and challenges.

Current Scenario

01

Currently, we have weather department and IMD alerts that are more focused on warning and containment rather than guidance on how to respond.


02

Air conditioning is one of the solutions, however access to it is expensive and not environment friendly.


03

The emissions resulting from use of non-renewable energy to power air conditioning can also create a propagating loop leading to more climate events.
